# 3.1 Order Book Structure



<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2025-06-09 at 2.54.14 PM.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

* **Depth Distribution:** Saudi stocks typically show thinner order books with steeper price impact curves
* **Quote Persistence:** Limit orders remain in the book 2.3x longer on average than in US markets
* **Price Impact:** Market orders of 0.1% of ADV create 3.5x more price impact than equivalent orders in developed markets
* **Queue Position Value:** First-in-line queue positions have 2.1x more execution probability than in developed markets
* **Cancel-to-Trade Ratio:** 4:1 compared to 10:1 in developed markets, indicating less quote spam

**Strategic Implication**

The Saudi market's order book structure creates significant alpha opportunities for sophisticated market makers who can:

1. Prioritize queue position acquisition during low-volatility periods
2. Model price impact more accurately than competitors
3. Provide liquidity selectively based on order book imbalance signals
4. Exploit the predictable behavior of retail traders around specific events
