# 4.1 Position Sizing

For a pair of stocks with spread Z<sub>t</sub> = X<sub>t</sub> - β·Y<sub>t</sub>:

1. LONG the spread: Buy 1 unit of X and short β units of Y
2. SHORT the spread: Short 1 unit of X and buy β units of Y

Position size should account for:

* Account volatility targeting
* Maximum drawdown constraints
* Liquidity limitations (especially for less liquid Saudi stocks)
* Beta-adjusted equal risk allocation across pairs
