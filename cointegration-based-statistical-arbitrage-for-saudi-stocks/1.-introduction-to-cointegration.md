# 1. Introduction to Cointegration

**Formal Definition**

Two time series X<sub>t</sub> and Y<sub>t</sub> are said to be cointegrated if:

1. Both X<sub>t</sub> and Y<sub>t</sub> are integrated of order d (denoted as I(d)), which typically means they are non-stationary time series ( in fact order d corresponds to the minimum number of differences required to obtain a covariance-stationary serie)
2. There exists a linear combination Z = X<sub>t</sub> - β·Y<sub>t</sub> that is integrated of order less than d, i.e (d-1), usually I(0) ''with X<sub>t</sub> and Y<sub>t</sub> being  I(1)'' , meaning it is stationary

***

Precisely the min number of differences required

in fact d-1

The equation Z<sub>t</sub> = X<sub>t</sub> - β·Y<sub>t</sub> forms the basis of our cointegration analysis, where:

* X<sub>t</sub> and Y<sub>t</sub> are the prices of two different Saudi stocks
* β is the cointegration coefficient (hedge ratio)
* Z<sub>t</sub> is the spread or residual series that should exhibit mean-reverting behavior

Z<sub>t</sub> = X<sub>t</sub> - β·Y<sub>t</sub>
