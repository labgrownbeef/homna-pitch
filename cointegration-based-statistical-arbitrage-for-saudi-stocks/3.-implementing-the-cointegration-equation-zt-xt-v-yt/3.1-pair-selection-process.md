# 3.1 Pair Selection Process

1. **Initial Filtering**: Begin with stocks in the same sector or with known fundamental relationships
2. **Stationarity Testing**: Use Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) tests to confirm non-stationarity of individual stocks
3. **Cointegration Testing**: Apply Johansen or Engle-Granger tests to identify cointegrated pairs
4. **Beta Calculation**: Estimate β using:
   * Ordinary Least Squares (OLS): β = cov(X<sub>t</sub>, Y<sub>t</sub>) / var(Y<sub>t</sub>)
   * Total Least Squares (TLS) for improved robustness
   * Dynamic estimation methods to account for time-varying relationships

**3.1.1 Focus on Saudi Banking Sector**

The banking sector exhibits strong cointegration relationships and offers promising opportunities for statistical arbitrage strategies in the Saudi market. Analysis of major Saudi banks shows notable characteristics:<br>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2025-06-08 at 10.01.47 PM.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The cointegration properties of Saudi bank pairs may be influenced by several structural factors:

* **Common Exposure Factors**: Correlation in interest rate sensitivity and market conditions
* **Regulatory Framework**: Similar regulatory requirements and compliance standards
* **Government Relationship**: Varying degrees of government involvement and relationships
* **Business Model Similarities**: Overlap in customer segments and product offerings
* **Seasonal Effects**: Potential impact of market cycles and calendar effects
